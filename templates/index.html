Mathematical Discussion

The Newton-Raphson method (also known as Newton's method) is a root-finding algorithm that produces successively better approximations to the roots (or zeroes) of a real-valued function. Given a function \( f(x) \) and its derivative \( f'(x) \), we begin with a first guess \( x_0 \) for a root of the function.

If the function satisfies sufficient assumptions and the initial guess is close, then a better approximation \( x_1 \) is:

\[ x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)} \]

Geometric Interpretation

Geometrically, \((x_{n+1}, 0)\) is the x-intercept of the tangent line to the graph of \( f \) at the point \((x_n, f(x_n))\). The process is repeated until a sufficiently precise value is reached.

Convergence Properties

Newton's method typically converges quadratically, meaning the number of correct digits roughly doubles with each step. However, it may fail to converge if the derivative is zero at an evaluation point or if the initial guess is too far from the actual root.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Finding \(\sqrt{2}\)

Find the positive root of \( f(x) = x^2 - 2 \).

1. Function & Derivative:
  • \( f(x) = x^2 - 2 \)
  • \( f'(x) = 2x \)
2. Iteration Formula: \[ x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{x_n^2 - 2}{2x_n} \]
3. Iterations (Starting with \( x_0 = 1 \)):
  • Iteration 1: \( x_1 = 1 - \frac{1^2 - 2}{2(1)} = 1.5 \)
  • Iteration 2: \( x_2 \approx 1.416667 \)
  • Iteration 3: \( x_3 \approx 1.414216 \)

✓ Converges to \(\sqrt{2} \approx 1.41421356\) very quickly.

Example 2: Solving \( x^3 - x - 2 = 0 \)

Find the real root of \( f(x) = x^3 - x - 2 \).

1. Function & Derivative:
  • \( f(x) = x^3 - x - 2 \)
  • \( f'(x) = 3x^2 - 1 \)
2. Iteration Formula: \[ x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{x_n^3 - x_n - 2}{3x_n^2 - 1} \]
3. Iterations (Starting with \( x_0 = 1.5 \)):
  • Iteration 1: \( x_1 \approx 1.521739 \)
  • Iteration 2: \( x_2 \approx 1.521380 \)
  • Iteration 3: \( x_3 \approx 1.521380 \)

✓ Root converges rapidly to \( \approx 1.521380 \).

Example 3: A Failing Case (Oscillation)

Consider \( f(x) = x^{1/3} \) with \( x_0 = 1 \).

1. Function & Derivative:
  • \( f(x) = x^{1/3} \)
  • \( f'(x) = \frac{1}{3}x^{-2/3} \)
2. Iteration Formula: \[ x_{n+1} = x_n - 3x_n = -2x_n \]
3. Iterations:
  • \( x_1 = -2 \), \( x_2 = 4 \), \( x_3 = -8 \)

✗ The sequence diverges! Newton-Raphson can fail with unfavorable functions.
